Description

This delicious meal is best made with old rice to ensure low moisture in the final product. This recipe makes a lot of food, but fried rice reheats well, making this a good meal prep candidate!

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups of dry rice (2 scoops of most rice cooker cups), cooked a day prior and kept overnight in the refrigerator
  • 1/4 cup of butter
  • 3 tbsp of sesame cooking oil
  • 32 oz. of frozen vegetables (I prefer a mix of peas, corn, and carrots)
  • 4 cloves of garlic; minced
  • 6 eggs
  • Soy Sauce to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pan over medium-high heat, add 1 tbsp of sesame oil and the frozen vegetables. Cook until vegetables are thawed and then add the minced garlic. Continue to cook until vegetables are soft and tender.
  2. Remove vegetables from pan.
  3. Melt 1 tbsp of butter in the pan. Once melted, crack the eggs directly into the pan. Season the eggs with salt and pepper and then scramble until they are thoroughly cooked.
  4. Remove eggs from pan.
  5. Melt remaining butter in pan. Add the rice and stir to coat.
  6. Fry the rice until almost all remaining moisture is gone and it starts to crisp up.
  7. Add soy sauce until desired flavor is achieved.
  8. Return vegetables and eggs to pan with rice, add remaining sesame oil and heat throughout.
  9. Serve!
