Carbonara

Description

Carbonara can be a difficult meal to learn, but once you get the hang of it you will be able to make a delicious, rich, and endlessly eatable pasta you can make any day of the week!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb of any pasta (preferably spaghetti like)
  • 6 oz. of bacon
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 4 oz. Pecorino Romano grated
  • 4 oz. Parmigiano Reggiano grated
  • Ground black pepper
  • Salt
  • 2 cloves of garlic

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot filled with water to a boil. Add salt.
  2. Begin boiling pasta according to package instructions. Make sure to reserve about 1/2 cup of pasta water when you finish boiling the pasta.
  3. In a bowl: mix together eggs, egg yolks, black pepper, and 3/4 of the grated cheese.
  4. Place bacon cut into about 1/2 inch pieces into a cold pan. Turn on burner to medium heat and fry until desired doneness. About 2 minutes before bacon is done cooking, add sliced garlic to pan to cook and crisp.
  5. Remove bacon and garlic from pan, but leave the bacon grease.
  6. Add strained pasta to the pan and toss to coat in the bacon grease. Turn off the stove and remove the pan from the heat.
  7. With the pan off the burner, add the egg slurry mixture to the pasta and quickly stir to ensure the eggs don't set. The remaining heat will cook the eggs.
  8. Slowly add the reserved pasta water until the sauce reaches the desire texture: smooth and glossy.
  9. Return the bacon and garlic to the pasta mix together, add salt to taste, and serve immediately.
